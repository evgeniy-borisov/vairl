Evolution of Mind, Metasystem Transitions, and Robot Swarms

June 23, 2026, 00:00 MSK

Draft — work in progress. Tracked in PA workspace (pub-evolution-mind-mst-swarm).

This post connects three lines of thought: how minds emerge from biological systems, how complexity jumps through metasystem transitions (MST), and how similar patterns appear in swarms of simple robots and software agents.

Pillar 1: Mind and biology

Ray Kurzweil’s How to Create a Mind emphasizes pattern recognition and hierarchical structure in the brain — a useful anchor for discussing intelligence as layered abstraction rather than a single module.

We will extract 3–5 theses and cite carefully (edition and chapter TBD).

Pillar 2: Metasystem transitions

Valentin Turchin’s The Phenomenon of Science frames evolution as a sequence of metasystem transitions — qualitative leaps when a new level of organization controls the level below. Science and reflective thought appear as late transitions in this framework.

Key questions for the post:

  • Where is the analogy between biological evolution and cognitive/technological evolution valid?
  • Where does the analogy break?

Pillar 3: Swarms and collectives

Swarm robotics and swarm intelligence study how many simple agents produce global behavior through local rules — self-organization without central control.

The bridge to MST: can a robot swarm or multi-agent system be understood as a new metasystem above individual agents?
